Intramuros: The Walled City of Manila

Stepping into Intramuros feels like traveling back in time to the Spanish colonial era of the Philippines. Surrounded by massive stone walls, this historic district in Manila is a treasure trove of cobblestone streets, century-old churches, and well-preserved forts. As you walk through its charming alleys, you’ll encounter architectural marvels like Fort Santiago, where national hero José Rizal was imprisoned, and San Agustin Church, a UNESCO-listed baroque masterpiece. The rhythmic sound of horse-drawn kalesas (carriages) echoes through the streets, adding to the old-world charm. Whether you're exploring on foot or riding a bamboo bike, Intramuros offers a captivating glimpse into the country’s rich past, blending history, culture, and romance in one unforgettable experience.

Chocolate Hills: Bohol’s Natural Wonder

The Chocolate Hills of Bohol are one of the most breathtaking and surreal landscapes in the Philippines. Spanning over 50 square kilometers, this geological wonder consists of more than 1,200 perfectly cone-shaped hills that turn chocolate brown during the dry season, resembling a field of giant chocolate mounds. While their exact origin remains a mystery, legends tell stories of giants, tears, and even playful duels that shaped the hills. Visitors can climb the viewpoint in Carmen for a panoramic view of this mesmerizing sight or explore the nearby areas on an ATV for an up-close adventure. Whether seen at sunrise, sunset, or under a blanket of stars, the Chocolate Hills leave every traveler in awe of nature’s artistry.

Allure Magazine to Launch Philippine Edition in 2025

Allure, the renowned beauty magazine, is set to debut Allure Philippines in March 2025. Rissa Mananquil Trillo, a celebrated figure in Philippine fashion and entrepreneurship, has been appointed as the editor-in-chief. Trillo aims to address misinformation in the local beauty industry by providing credible and authentic content that resonates with Filipino readers, blending global insights with local relevance. This initiative seeks to celebrate Filipino beauty and talent, enhancing the country's presence on the global beauty stage.

Philippines Releases Comic Book to Educate Youth on South China Sea Dispute

In an effort to counter disinformation and educate young Filipinos about maritime rights, the Philippine government has launched a 40-page comic book titled "The Stories of Teacher Jun." Aimed at students and remote communities, the comic uses simple language and colorful illustrations to present the Philippine perspective on the South China Sea disputes. National Security Adviser Eduardo Año emphasized the need to combat misleading narratives, highlighting this initiative as part of broader efforts to uphold transparency and truth regarding the country's territorial rights.
